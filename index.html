Michael Osgood

My name is Michael Osgood and I am a huge science and tech enthusiast. I am a full-stack developer. I have completed a comprehensive full-stack coding program through the University of California Irvine. I also work as a User Acceptance Tester for an innovative software company in Irvine, California.

When I was 20 years old, I enlisted in the U.S Air Force. And no, I was not a pilot. I worked in the medical field, more specifically Public Health. This career field spurred my fascination with biology. So after four years in the Air Force, I moved to Boulder, Colorado to study Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at the University of Colorado. During that time, I studied a myriad of topics to include biology, evolution, genetics, ecology, chemistry, physics, human anatomy, and physiology. During college, I worked as a Support Analyst for a software company. After graduating in 2015, I moved to California and continued to work on the operations side of software for tech companies.

After 3 years of working on the client-facing side of software, I moved to the Product Management side as a User Acceptance Tester. For this role, I work with the developers to test and troubleshoot software before it goes to production. I have gained extensive knowledge and practice in developing and testing software with the latest technologies through the full-stack coding bootcamp. The coursework covered many topics from HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, React.js, MySQL and many other tools that have helped me become a full-stack developer.
